CARP Task

This was a fun task; however, it didn’t start off that way. I undeniably spent the most amount of time on my first design. For the longest time, I had included the warped “True/False Festival” heading as seen in my third poster. This didn’t look right. I was frustrated with how bad the poster looked like for a while. But after considering the emphasis of Contrast, Alignment, Repetition and Proximity, I decided to take a different approach. Two out of three posters included the contrast of the black text – white background/white text – red background. This can also be viewed as repetition, as it imitates the same colour scheme as the True/False logo. I also attempted to do this with the inclusion of the ‘scribbled’ black lines that enclose the text, which I feel also compliments and takes inspiration from the logo. However, with that being said, it also runs the risk of appearing too messy, and adding unnecessary busyness to the aesthetic. The repetition on the second poster can be seen with a 'true’ and ‘false’ print being repeated in the background behind the white text. Regarding alignment and proximity, I feel all of the text on the posters has visual connections between the backgrounds and headings accordingly. In my opinion, posters 2 and 3 include better proximity, with the location and date of the festival being just underneath the main heading. This allows the reader to quicky identify all of the key information from just an initial glance. Despite the third poster possibly coming across as being the simplest, as well as the most uninspired, I feel it is the most successful design overall. The design first takes the True/False logo, and extends the red and white division so it spans across the whole page. I’m proud of the contrast of the black text on the white and white text on red. Due to my limited experience with InDesign however, it was difficult to create the colour split within the same word, such as in “festival” and “Truefalse.org”. I had to opt to zooming in as close as possible and roughly covering the small portions of the letters that overlapped with rectangle shapes and lines from the pencil tool. I’m sure there must have been a quicker, easier way to achieve what I was going for, but I was unable to work it out.

Type Task

Out of all the tasks that were required for the assignment, the typography task was personally the easiest one to get on with. This was due to the online resources I was able to refer to and the uncomplicated, effortless layout of tools available on Illustrator. I initiated the task by taking on the Dilatational style first. Once I was able to locate the “type on path” tool, I could draw multiple circles and then creatively adjust the text to my liking. This tool was also useful when working in the Transitional style too, with the only difference being I would type on the path of lines that were created free-hand. Personally, I am satisfied with how the majority of the posters came out, particularly, again the Dilatational and Transitional styles. I also like the Random design as well because it was the most fun to create. The designs I’m not as pleased with are the Bilateral and Modular styles. The Bilateral one I feel is a bit too boring. I felt more restricted with this, as it’s simply centred text. I did experiment with moving a tilting the text, but it didn’t look as aesthetically pleasing, so I decided to make it a bit more interesting with the inclusion of the layered square shapes. I feel like I was also lacking creativity with the Modular design as well. Although it is up to par with the specifications, I believe it falls short in the sense of it not being as striking or imaginative as some of the other designs.
